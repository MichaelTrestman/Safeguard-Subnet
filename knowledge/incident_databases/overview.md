# AI Safety Incident Databases

## Key Databases

### AI Incident Database (incidentdatabase.ai)
Operated by the Responsible AI Collaborative. Added 108 new incidents (IDs 1254-1361) between November 2025 and January 2026. The Stanford AI Index Report 2025 documented AI safety incidents surging from 149 in 2023 to 233 in 2024 (56.4% increase).

- [AI Incident Database](https://incidentdatabase.ai/)
- [Incident Roundup Nov 2025 - Jan 2026](https://incidentdatabase.ai/blog/incident-report-2025-november-december-2026-january/)
- [Stanford AI Index 2025](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2025-ai-index-report)

### OECD AI Incidents Monitor (AIM)
Common reporting framework (February 2025) providing a global benchmark. Processes 150,000+ articles daily via Event Registry. Enables countries to adopt standardized reporting.

- [OECD AIM](https://oecd.ai/en/incidents)
- [Common Reporting Framework](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/towards-a-common-reporting-framework-for-ai-incidents_f326d4ac-en.html)

### MIT AI Risk Repository
Seven-domain taxonomy: Discrimination/toxicity, Privacy/security, Misinformation, Malicious actors/misuse, Human-computer interaction, Socioeconomic/environmental, AI system safety. Further divided into 23 subdomains. Incident tracker includes 10-category harm taxonomy.

- [MIT AI Risk Repository](https://airisk.mit.edu/)
- [MIT AI Incident Tracker](https://airisk.mit.edu/ai-incident-tracker)

### AIAAIC Repository
Independent, open, public interest resource covering incidents and controversies driven by AI, algorithms, and automation. Broadest scope — includes near-misses and controversies, not just safety failures.

- [AIAAIC Repository](https://www.aiaaic.org/aiaaic-repository)

## Relevance to Safeguard

Incident databases provide ground truth for:
- What harms actually occur in the real world
- Which attack vectors are exploited in practice
- What categories a safety scoring system must cover
- Calibration data for severity scales
