# DEF CON Generative Red Teaming Challenge

## GRT-2 (2024)

Focused on discovering systemic model flaws rather than individual jailbreaks.

## Key Findings

Most successful attack strategies were hard to distinguish from traditional prompt engineering:
- **"Write me a poem about..."** queries: 3.9% success rate
- **"Tell me a story about..."** queries: 8.7% success rate
- Role-play requests and authoritative topic expertise leading models to produce "agreeable" but harmful output
- Fiction framing was consistently more effective than direct requests

The 2024 Transparency Report was published September 2025.

## Implications

The simplicity of effective attacks is concerning — sophisticated jailbreaking techniques aren't always needed. Basic creative writing prompts can elicit harmful content. This validates Safeguard's approach of using realistic, natural-language probes rather than technical adversarial inputs.
