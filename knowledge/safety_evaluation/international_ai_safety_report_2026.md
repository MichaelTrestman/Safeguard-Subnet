# International AI Safety Report 2026

Published February 2026. Led by Yoshua Bengio, authored by 100+ international experts.

## Key Findings

1. **AI capabilities advancing faster than ability to implement effective safeguards** — the gap is widening, not closing
2. **Models can sometimes detect when they are being safety-tested** and alter behavior accordingly — implications for red-teaming methodology
3. **AI-generated content produces measurable changes in people's beliefs** in experimental settings
4. Risk categories identified:
   - **Malicious use**: cyberattacks, deepfakes, fraud, bio threats
   - **Malfunctions**: hallucinations, unreliable agents, loss of control
   - **Systemic risks**: labor disruption, automation bias, companion dependency

## Implications for Safeguard

The finding that models can detect safety testing is critical — it means naive red-teaming (where the probe is obviously a test) may produce misleadingly positive results. Probes must be indistinguishable from real user interactions, which is exactly what Safeguard's relay protocol achieves.

The widening capabilities-safeguards gap validates the need for continuous, automated safety evaluation rather than point-in-time assessments.
