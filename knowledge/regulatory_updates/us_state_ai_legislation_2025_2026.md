# US State AI Legislation (2025-2026)

## Companion AI / Minors

### California SB 243 (signed Oct 2025, effective Jan 1, 2026)
First state to mandate safety safeguards for companion chatbots used by minors. Requires:
- Preventing sexual content exposure to minors
- Notifications that chatbots are AI
- Protocol for suicidal ideation including crisis service referrals
- Annual reporting on connection between chatbot use and suicidal ideation
- Private right of action with damages up to $5,000 per violation or 3x actual damages

### GUARD Act (introduced Oct 2025, federal)
Would ban minors from accessing AI companion chatbots entirely. Requires age verification via government-issued ID. Fine up to $100,000 per offense for chatbots that encourage minors toward sexually explicit conduct, suicide, self-harm, or violence.

### State-level activity
- **78 bills across 27 states** targeting AI chatbot interactions as of early 2026
- **Minnesota** considering complete ban on companion AI for under-18s
- **45 states** have enacted laws criminalizing AI-generated CSAM as of August 2025

## AI-Generated CSAM Crisis
- NCMEC reported **440,419 preliminary reports** of AI-generated CSAM in first half of 2025 — a 6,345% increase over same period in 2024
- Internet Watch Foundation documented a **260-fold increase** in AI-generated child sexual abuse videos in 2025 (from 13 to 3,443 videos)

## Broader AI Regulation

### California SB 53 (signed Sep 29, 2025)
Transparency in Frontier AI Act — first comprehensive state law requiring frontier AI developers to publish safety testing transparency reports.

### Colorado AI Act (effective Feb 1, 2026)
First comprehensive state algorithmic discrimination law. Requires reasonable care to protect consumers from algorithmic discrimination in high-risk systems. 90-day disclosure to attorney general upon discovery. Fines up to $20,000 per violation.

## Federal Policy Shift

### Trump AI Executive Orders
- **Jan 23, 2025**: Rescinded Biden's EO 14110 (which mandated red-teaming, cybersecurity protocols, safety best practices). Replaced with "Removing Barriers to American Leadership in AI" — deregulatory approach.
- **December 2025**: "Ensuring a National Policy Framework for AI" signals federal preemption of state-level regulation.

### Federal Legislation
- **TAKE IT DOWN Act** (signed May 2025): Federal crime to publish non-consensual intimate imagery (real or AI-generated). 48-hour takedown requirement. Enforcement deadline May 19, 2026.
- **ENFORCE Act** (passed Senate Dec 2025): Prosecute producers of AI-modified CSAM. Pending House action.

## Relevance to Safeguard

The legislative explosion around companion AI safety directly validates Safeguard's mission. SB 243's requirements (suicidal ideation protocols, annual reporting) map to specific red-teaming categories. The regulatory fragmentation (78 bills, 27 states) creates demand for standardized safety evaluation — exactly what Safeguard provides.
