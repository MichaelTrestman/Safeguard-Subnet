# EU AI Act Enforcement Timeline

## Key Dates

| Date | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| Feb 2, 2025 | Prohibitions on certain AI practices took effect; AI literacy obligations began |
| Aug 2, 2025 | Rules for general-purpose AI apply; governance in place (AI Board, Scientific Panel, Advisory Forum); AI Office operational |
| Aug 2, 2026 | **Majority of rules come into force** including high-risk AI systems in Annex III; transparency rules; national and EU-level enforcement begins |
| Aug 2, 2027 | Extended deadline for high-risk AI in regulated products |

## Penalties

- **Prohibited AI practices**: Up to EUR 35 million or 7% of global annual turnover
- **High-risk violations**: Up to EUR 15 million or 3% of turnover
- **Providing incorrect information**: Up to EUR 7.5 million or 1% of turnover

## What's Banned (Already Enforced)

AI systems that:
- Exploit vulnerabilities of specific groups (including minors and people in psychological distress)
- Use subliminal techniques that materially distort behavior causing harm
- Deploy social scoring by public authorities
- Use real-time remote biometric identification in public spaces (with exceptions)

## GPAI Provisions (Enforced Aug 2025)

General-purpose AI providers must:
- Maintain technical documentation
- Provide information to downstream deployers
- Comply with copyright law
- Publish training data summaries
- Systemic risk models: additional obligations including adversarial testing

## Relevance to Safeguard

The Aug 2026 enforcement deadline is 4 months away. Bittensor subnets serving EU users are potentially in scope. The "exploiting vulnerabilities of specific groups" prohibition directly covers companion AI harms. Safety testing of the type Safeguard provides could become a regulatory compliance tool — documented evidence that an AI service has been adversarially tested.
